GENERAL RULES

Cats can be transported in cars without special restrictions if secured safely.
Dogs can be transported in cars. Large dogs should be restrained.
Small animals are usually allowed in train cabins inside carriers.
Large dogs are allowed in trains but may require a separate ticket and leash.
Airplanes allow small animals under approximately 8 kg in cabin carriers.
Animals above 8 kg are typically transported in airplane cargo compartments.
Some airlines restrict brachycephalic breeds from air transport.
Birds can be transported in carriers in cars and trains.
Rodents such as hamsters and guinea pigs can be transported in carriers.
Reptiles may require special permission depending on transport provider.


TIME ESTIMATION RULES

Cars typically travel at average speeds of 60–90 km/h.
Trains typically travel at average speeds of 80–160 km/h.
Airplanes travel at approximately 700–900 km/h, but airport procedures add 2–4 hours.


TRANSPORT LIMITATIONS

Very large animals cannot be transported in airplane cabins.
All animals transported by air require carriers.
Animals must be secured during car transport.
Train transport usually requires carriers or leash.
